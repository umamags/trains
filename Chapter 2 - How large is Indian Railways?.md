## 1. Track Length

Indian Railways operates about **69,000 km of route**, but because many busy corridors have double, triple or quadruple tracks, the **total running track exceeds 132,000 km**.

To put that into perspective:

- 🌍 Earth's circumference is about **40,075 km**
    
- Indian Railways' running track could wrap around the Earth **more than three times**.
    

That makes it one of the largest railway networks on the planet.

---

## 2. Stations

There are approximately **7,300 railway stations**.

That means:

- one station every **9–10 km of route**
    
- from Himalayan hill stations to desert outposts
    
- from megacities handling half a million passengers daily to tiny rural halts where only a handful of trains stop.
    

---

## 3. Passenger Traffic

Every day, Indian Railways carries roughly **24 million passengers**.

That is comparable to the **entire population of Australia** traveling **every single day**.

Over a year, that's nearly **9 billion passenger journeys**.

For comparison:

|Comparison|Scale|
|---|---|
|Australia|~27 million people|
|Switzerland|~9 million people|
|Daily Indian Railways passengers|~24 million|

Every sunrise, the railway effectively moves an entire medium-sized nation.

---

## 4. Freight

Passenger trains are only half the story.

Indian Railways transports around **1.6 billion tonnes of freight each year**, including:

- Coal
    
- Food grains
    
- Steel
    
- Cement
    
- Petroleum
    
- Containers
    
- Fertilizers
    
- Automobiles
    

Nearly **half of all railway freight is coal**, which keeps many of India's power plants supplied.

---

## 5. Employees

With approximately **1.2 million employees**, Indian Railways is among the world's largest employers.

Its workforce includes:

- Drivers
    
- Guards
    
- Station Masters
    
- Signal operators
    
- Track maintainers
    
- Engineers
    
- Electrical technicians
    
- Coach inspectors
    
- Bridge engineers
    
- Catering staff
    
- Doctors
    
- Teachers
    
- IT professionals
    
- Security personnel
    

It is essentially a city-sized workforce dedicated to keeping trains moving around the clock.

---

## 6. Locomotives

Indian Railways owns more than **15,000 locomotives**, including:

- Electric locomotives
    
- Diesel locomotives
    
- Heritage steam locomotives
    

The network is rapidly becoming electrified, with the vast majority of passenger and freight traffic now hauled by electric engines.

### A simple way to visualize it

Imagine waking up tomorrow and transporting:

- almost the **entire population of Australia**,
    
- using **over 15,000 locomotives**,
    
- stopping at **7,300 stations**,
    
- across enough railway track to circle the Earth **three times**,
    
- while moving **millions of tonnes of freight** that keep power plants running, factories supplied, and food reaching markets.
    

That is the scale at which Indian Railways operates—every single day.
